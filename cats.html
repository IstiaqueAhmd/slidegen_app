Feline Biology

Sensory Superpowers

Cats have 200 million scent receptors and can rotate ears 180Â° to detect prey with incredible precision.

Flexible Skeletons

With 230 bones (humans have 206), their collarbones aren't connected allowing extreme flexibility.

Specialized Paws

Paw pads provide shock absorption and silent movement, while retractable claws maintain sharpness.

Cat Communication

Vocalizations

Cats have over 100 vocal sounds including specialized meows only for humans.

Tail Signals

Upright tail indicates confidence, puffed tail shows fear, slow wag means focus.

Body Language

Slow blinks show trust, exposed belly indicates vulnerability, kneading demonstrates contentment.

Evolutionary Journey

  • Descended from African wildcats 10,000 years ago
  • Domesticated in Fertile Crescent for pest control
  • Spread globally via ancient trade routes
  • Worshipped in Egyptian society as divine

Creating Cat-Friendly Homes

1

Vertical Territory

Install cat trees, shelves, and perches at multiple heights to satisfy climbing instincts

2

Sensory Enrichment

Provide puzzle feeders, cat grass, and window perches for mental stimulation

3

Litter Management

Maintain multiple uncovered boxes (n+1 rule) with unscented litter in quiet locations

4

Safe Outdoor Access

Create catios or use harness training for secure outdoor experiences
