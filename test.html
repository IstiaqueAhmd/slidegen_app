MANGO

The Golden King of Fruits

Botanical Profile

Scientific Classification

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Family: Anacardiaceae
  • Genus: Mangifera
  • Species: Indica

Physical Attributes

  • Height: Up to 130 feet
  • Lifespan: 100+ years
  • Leaf longevity: Up to 5 years
  • Fruit weight: 150g-2kg

Reproductive Traits

  • Flowering time: Winter months
  • Fruit maturity: 3-6 months
  • Pollination: Insects & wind
  • Yield: 200-300 fruits/tree

Health Benefits

Vision Boost

Rich in Vitamin A and beta-carotene, mangoes reduce night blindness and dry eyes

Heart Health

Magnesium and potassium help regulate blood pressure and prevent CVDs

Brain Function

Glutamine acid enhances concentration and memory retention abilities

Digestive Aid

Enzymes like amylases break down proteins and improve gut health

Cultural Significance

  • Symbol of love in Hindu mythology - associated with Kama, god of love
  • Used in religious ceremonies across India, Philippines, and Thailand
  • Designated as Pakistan's national fruit and India's national tree
  • Featured in classical artworks and Mughal miniature paintings
  • Traditional folk remedy in Ayurvedic medicine for centuries

Mango Varietal Diversity

Haden

Florida-born hybrid with vibrant red-green skin and rich aromatic flavor

Alphonso

Prized Indian export with saffron-colored pulp and buttery texture

Tommy Atkins

Commercial favorite with excellent shelf life and firm flesh

Culinary Action Guide

1

Selection & Ripeness

Choose fruit with yellowish-red skin and sweet aroma. Press gently near stem - should give slightly

2

Cutting Techniques

Cut down each side of the flat seed. Score flesh into cubes then scoop out with spoon

3

Flavor Pairings

Complement with lime/chili powder, coconut milk, yogurt, fresh mint or basil

4

Preservation
